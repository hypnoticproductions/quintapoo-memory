### SIG-JAN26-002: Uganda Tops Regional Growth at 6.4% (World Bank)
**Source:** Watchdog Uganda (5 days ago)
**Signal:** Uganda projected to grow 6.4% in 2026, outpacing Kenya (4.9%), Tanzania (6.2%), DRC (5.1%). Only Rwanda faster at 7.2%. Built on 2025's 6.3% growth (highest since COVID). Driven by household consumption, government spending, coffee exports.

**Morphic Fit:** Resource-rich nations converting commodity advantages into structural growth. Coffee as strategic export (recall SIG-001 from Jan 21: Uganda coffee exports $2.4B).

### SIG-JAN26-003: Ghana Gold Exports Double to $10B
**Source:** Energy Capital Power (9 hours ago)
**Signal:** Ghana's artisanal/small-scale gold mining (ASGM) sector generated $10 billion in export revenue in 2025, up from $5 billion in 2024. Cedi ranked 2nd-best performing currency globally in 2025. ASGM employs 1M+ people, accounts for majority of Ghana's export revenue.
**Trade Angle:** State-backed reforms + international partnerships (UN planetGOLD initiative) driving formalization. Ghana GoldBod (official domestic gold buyer) launched May 2025, exceeded $5B trading in first 5 months. Record gold prices + employment agenda = sector expansion.
**Morphic Fit:** Informal economy formalization creates export velocity. Small-scale mining at scale = national economic stabilizer.

### SIG-JAN26-004: Africa-CARICOM Trade Could Hit $1B
**Source:** CARICOM Private Sector Organization (6 days ago)
**Signal:** CPSO study shows Africa Union exports to CARICOM could surpass $1 billion. 13 African countries can each supply $58M+ in CSME non-mineral fuel imports at 60% of current prices. 579 product lines identified with competitive pricing from 4+ African suppliers per product.
**Trade Angle:** Supply chain rewiring driven by US protectionism and geopolitical uncertainty. CARICOM MSMEs seeking alternative sourcing. Transport connectivity, logistics costs, payment systems remain constraints.
**Morphic Fit:** South-South trade as strategic imperative. Africa-Caribbean corridor bypasses traditional North-South dependencies.

### SIG-JAN26-005: African Tech Funding Hits $4.1B (Debt Surge)
**Source:** Daba Finance, Ecofin Agency (2-3 days ago)
**Signal:** African tech startups raised $4.1 billion in 2025 ($2.4B equity, $1.6B debt). Debt financing hit record 41% of total capital. Kenya led debt funding. 635+ disclosed deals. Equity remained stable while debt surged.
**Trade Angle:** Shift from venture capital to debt financing signals maturity. Startups generating revenue, seeking growth capital vs. early-stage equity. Banks, telecoms, corporates acquiring startups (rising M&A activity).
**Morphic Fit:** African tech ecosystem maturing beyond unicorn hype. Revenue-generating businesses accessing non-dilutive capital.

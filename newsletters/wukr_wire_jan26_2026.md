## Today's Top 5 Signals

### 1. Trinidad & Tobago: $1B Bond Oversubscribed 2.5x

On January 22, 2026, Trinidad & Tobago issued a **10-year, $1 billion sovereign bond** on the US market. The order book exceeded **$2.4 billion** with 144 investors—more than double the final issue size. The bond priced 54.6 basis points tighter than the country's 2016 issuance, despite two negative rating outlooks.

**Trade Angle:** International investor confidence in Caribbean credit fundamentals. Debt refinancing extends average life from 4.1 to 6.3 years. Small nations accessing capital markets on increasingly favorable terms.

**Morphic Fit:** Small nations leveraging fiscal discipline to compete for global capital. Investor appetite for Caribbean sovereign debt signals risk perception shift.

### 2. Uganda Tops Regional Growth at 6.4%

Uganda is set to outpace most of its East African neighbors in 2026, according to the World Bank. The country is projected to grow **6.4%**, slightly above Tanzania (6.2%) and well ahead of Kenya (4.9%). Only Rwanda, at 7.2%, is expected to grow faster in the region.

**Drivers:** Household consumption, government spending, and coffee exports. Uganda's coffee sector exported **$2.4 billion** in 2025, benefiting from favorable global prices.

**Morphic Fit:** Resource-rich nations converting commodity advantages into sustained growth. Coffee as strategic export (recall SIG-001 from Jan 21: Uganda coffee exports $2.4B).

### 3. Ghana Gold Exports Double to $10B

Ghana's artisanal and small-scale gold mining (ASGM) sector generated **$10 billion** in export revenue in 2025, up from **$5 billion** in 2024. The surge in gold exports strengthened Ghana's macroeconomic position, with the cedi ranking as the **second-best performing currency globally** in 2025.

**What Changed:** State-backed reforms + international partnerships (UN planetGOLD initiative). Ghana GoldBod (official domestic gold buyer) launched May 2025, exceeded **$5 billion** trading in first 5 months.

**Morphic Fit:** Informal economy formalization creates export velocity. Small-scale mining at scale = national economic stabilizer.

### 4. Africa-CARICOM Trade Could Hit $1B

A recent study by the CARICOM Private Sector Organization (CPSO) revealed that **Africa Union exports to CARICOM could surpass $1 billion**. The analysis identified **579 product lines** where African suppliers can competitively price goods at **60% of current benchmarks**.

**Strategic Context:** Supply chain rewiring driven by US protectionism and geopolitical uncertainty. CARICOM MSMEs seeking alternative sourcing. Transport connectivity, logistics costs, payment systems remain constraints.

**Morphic Fit:** South-South trade as strategic imperative. Africa-Caribbean corridor bypasses traditional North-South dependencies.

### 5. African Tech Funding Hits $4.1B (Debt Surge)

African tech startups raised **$4.1 billion** in 2025 ($2.4B equity, $1.6B debt). Debt financing hit record **41% of total capital**. Kenya led debt funding. 635+ disclosed deals.

**What This Means:** Shift from venture capital to debt financing signals maturity. Startups generating revenue, seeking growth capital vs. early-stage equity. Banks, telecoms, corporates acquiring startups (rising M&A activity).

**Morphic Fit:** African tech ecosystem maturing beyond unicorn hype. Revenue-generating businesses accessing non-dilutive capital.
